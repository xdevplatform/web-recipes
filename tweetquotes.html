#Recipe: Tweet Quotes

Give your readers timely, relevant quotes to share on Twitter.

Tweet quotes enable your readers to share content on your behalf, increasing your distribution on Twitter. And by adding Twitter Cards you site, you offer a more compelling experience to Twitter users, as well as gain insight and analytics to help optimize your conversion and traffic.

This recipe can be built into your website or CMS system in under 10 minutes. It uses and free fully supported Twitter tools, including:

  • Web Intents
  • Twitter Cards
  • Twitter Analytics

The code and the how-to for you to get started (including this recipe) is all below.

Step 1: Learn about Web Intents  

Step 2: Learn about Web Intents

A Web Intent helps readers share your message on Twitter, without ever leaving your site.

A Web Intent is a paramterized URL that, when clicked, pre-populates your message in a tweet. The most popular parameters are:

  • text. The text/summary quote you want your readers to share.
  • url. The URL of your content on your site.
  • via. The Twitter handle associated with your site.
  • related. A comma separated list of Twitter handles related to your site.

Below is a sample Web Intent URL:

Copy 
https://twitter.com/intent/tweet?text=This%is%20a%20tweet%20quote&url=http%3A%2F%2Fdev.twitter.com&via=twitterdev

If you want additional customization options or power tips, read more on Twitter Web Intents.

Step 3: Compose a Twitter Card

Twitter Cards bring rich photos and summary to a bigger audience.

When your readers Tweet links to your articles, their Tweets will also have the photo and summary you specify.

Add the below HTML to the HEAD section of your website:

Copy 
<meta name="twitter:card" content="summary_large_image" />
<meta name="twitter:site" content="@flickr" />
<meta name="twitter:title" content="publ(ic) mark(et)" />
<meta name="twitter:description" content="Pike Place Market  Seattle, WA USA" />
<meta name="twitter:image" content="http://farm4.staticflickr.com/3361/3433069736_7193f22ab4_z.jpg" />
<meta name="twitter:url" content="https://www.flickr.com/photos/secret_canadian/3433069736//" />

For advanced options, read the Twitter Card Documentation.

Step 4: Activate your Twitter Card

Use the Card Validator to preview and activate your Twitter Card.

Once you are satisfied with the photo and the content of your Card, you must request approval on the Validator by filling out your site and user details.

A few more notes about Card approval:

  1. Cards are whitelisted on a Card-per-domain basis.
  2. Different Card types need to be approved separately for the same domain.
  3. Card approval is typically granted within minutes of submission.

Step 5: Optimize performance on Twitter Analytics

Twitter Analytics provides a 360 view of your website integration with Twitter.

The Cards Dashboard summarizes your integration's performance in terms of website traffic, engagement and app installs (if applicable). Additionally, you can see which content is the most successful, and the influencers who help you reach the most people.

After logging into Twitter Analytics, click on the "Twitter Cards" link at the top to get started.

Experiment with different Card types, photos and text summaries to see how they perform. Then optimize your tweet quotes and Cards for better distribution and traffic back to your site.
